# GEO Agency - Design Directions v1

Updated: 2026-03-13

These are four genuinely different strategic directions, not four color variants.
Each one assumes a different primary buyer signal and conversion strategy.

---

## Direction 1 — AI Visibility Command Center

**Label:** Profound-inspired  
**Best use case:** When the buyer already knows they have an AI visibility problem and wants to see a system, not a pitch.  
**Buyer profile:** CTOs, CMOs, digital directors in companies where AI-generated answers are already a topic on the radar.

### What makes it different

- Sells control and measurement, not vague SEO uplift
- Dashboard above the fold — the product is visible before the pitch
- Numeric proof is the main trust mechanism (percentages, citations, visibility scores)
- Pricing is secondary to the demo CTA

### Look and feel

- Dark background, near-black (#0D0F12 or similar)
- Sharp contrast typography (white + accent cyan or electric blue)
- Cards that look like real product surfaces
- Sparse layout with intentional spacing
- Logo strip with enterprise names

### Homepage structure

1. Hero: "Control how AI sees your brand" + one dominant CTA
2. Trusted by [logos]
3. The problem: AI search is already deciding who gets cited
4. The platform: 3 service surfaces as product cards
5. Sample report preview (blurred or watermarked)
6. One case study with a numeric lift
7. Pricing
8. Support path
9. Final CTA: "Launch my GEO audit"

### Watch out

If the product is still 100% service-led, this direction creates a platform
expectation that must be managed. Mitigate with clear "done-for-you" language
on every product card.

### Build prompt

Build a premium dark-themed landing page for a GEO agency that positions itself
as an AI visibility control center. Use a near-black background, sharp
typography, and card-style surfaces that evoke a real product dashboard. The
page sells a productized service — not software — but uses the language and
visual grammar of enterprise SaaS. Include: hero with dominant CTA ("Launch my
GEO audit"), logo strip, problem statement, 3 service surfaces as cards,
blurred sample report, one case study card with a numeric lift, pricing section
with 3 tiers, support path, FAQ, final CTA. Avoid purple. Avoid generic AI
marketing copy. Every claim must feel defensible.

## Direction 2 — High-Velocity Growth Agency

**Label:** NoGood-inspired  
**Best use case:** When the buyer has a conversion bias — they respond to social proof, ROI signals, and commercial momentum before they want detail.  
**Buyer profile:** Growth leads, startup founders, e-commerce directors who are used to buying agency services and want results fast.

### What makes it different

- Social proof is the first trust mechanism, not product features
- CTA is repeated at least three times in the first scroll
- The site feels like a sales motion, not an editorial
- Logos and testimonials load fast and dominate above the fold

### Look and feel

- Dark but warmer (deep navy or dark graphite, not pure black)
- Oversized typography for section titles
- Bold CTA blocks with high-contrast buttons
- Dense case-study cards with real results
- Logos placed high

### Homepage structure

1. Hero: Strong ROI promise + CTA
2. Logo strip + one trust stat
3. First CTA repeat
4. Service pillars (3 cards, no jargon)
5. Case study block (2–3 cards with outcome headlines)
6. Proof stats bar
7. Pricing ladder
8. Second CTA repeat
9. FAQ
10. Final CTA

### Watch out

This direction relies on having real proof. Without at least 2–3 solid case
results, it reads as empty swagger. Placeholder case studies must be clearly
marked or replaced with a pre-launch waitlist narrative.

### Build prompt

Create a conversion-heavy agency landing page with a bold dark look (deep
navy, not black), oversized typography, dense social proof, and a repeated CTA
rhythm. The product is a GEO service for AI search visibility and LLM citation
optimization. Include: hero with ROI promise, logo strip, service pillars (3
cards), 2–3 case study cards with outcome headlines, proof stats bar, pricing
with 3 tiers, FAQ, and three CTA blocks across the page. All claims must be
defensible. Testimonials may be placeholder-labeled for now. Make it feel like
a serious growth agency, not a startup side project.

## Direction 3 — Executive Trust and Thought Leadership

**Label:** First Page Sage-inspired  
**Best use case:** When the buyer needs to trust the person behind the agency before they buy the service. Best matched to Alexis's founder profile.  
**Buyer profile:** Executive buyers, legal and finance-adjacent companies, B2B founders who make deliberate decisions and resist hard sells.

### What makes it different

- The founder is the trust mechanism, not the brand
- Process and deliverables get as much space as the pitch
- Reads more like an executive brief than a startup homepage
- Support and QA are named explicitly — this signals commitment, not just capability

### Look and feel

- Light background (off-white or warm gray)
- Restrained serif display font paired with a clean sans body
- Wide editorial margins
- No flashy motion, subtle transitions only
- Founder presence possible (photo or note)

### Homepage structure

1. Hero: Positioning statement + why now paragraph
2. Why this matters now (2–3 lines, no bullet walls)
3. How we work (process steps, not service names)
4. Sample deliverables with honest descriptions
5. Pricing with context
6. Support and QA section (explicitly named)
7. FAQ
8. Founder note or short bio
9. CTA: "Book a 30-minute scoping call"

### Watch out

This direction is the hardest to convert cold traffic with. It works best when
backed by a content flywheel (Substack, LinkedIn, word of mouth) that
pre-qualifies the buyer. Without content, the site has no acquisition driver.

### Build prompt

Design a calm, premium B2B website for a GEO agency. The tone should feel like
an executive advisory firm with operational rigor — not a loud startup. Use a
light palette (warm off-white background), one serif display font paired with a
clean sans body. Build sections for: hero with positioning statement, why-now
paragraph, process steps (not marketing names), sample deliverables with honest
descriptions, transparent pricing with context, support and QA section, FAQ,
short founder note, and a single CTA to book a scoping call. No bold promises.
No empty superlatives. Every sentence should sound like someone with 15 years
of operator experience wrote it.

## Direction 4 — Friendly AI-Native Operator

**Label:** Scrunch-inspired  
**Best use case:** When the buyer is an early adopter who lives in AI tools and wants a diagnostic before they commit. Also the right direction if a SaaS hybrid is in the roadmap.  
**Buyer profile:** Digital-native founders, product teams, SaaS CMOs who want to understand their AI visibility baseline before buying.

### What makes it different

- Starts with a diagnostic or benchmark concept, not a sales pitch
- Score and visibility cards give the buyer a tangible starting point
- Feels like a product company that also does services, not the reverse
- More accessible tone and visual energy than the other three

### Look and feel

- Neutral light background with subtle gradients (not dark)
- Bright accent cards
- Score and benchmark visuals ("Your AI visibility score: 34/100")
- Product-style screenshots with soft depth shadows
- Human tone in headers

### Homepage structure

1. Hero: "Find out how AI sees your brand" + free audit CTA
2. Benchmark card visual (sample score or visibility grid)
3. How AI search has changed discovery (short explainer)
4. Our services (3 cards: audit, optimize, monitor)
5. Sample audit preview
6. Partner or client logos
7. Pricing
8. Support CTA
9. Final CTA: "Get your free visibility score"

### Watch out

The free-audit hook creates strong top-of-funnel pull but requires a real
deliverable or it disappoints immediately. Either build a lightweight real
audit, or adjust CTA to "Request a sample report" as a placeholder.

### Build prompt

Generate a modern AI-native landing page for a GEO agency that combines a
benchmark product feel with a high-touch service offer. Use a light neutral
background with subtle gradients, bright accent cards, and polished
product-style screenshots. Open with a diagnostic hook ("Find out how AI sees
your brand"), then show a sample benchmark card or visibility score visual.
Build sections for: how AI search has changed discovery, 3 service cards
(audit, optimize, monitor), sample audit preview, client logos, pricing (3
tiers), support CTA, FAQ, and a final CTA: "Get your free visibility score."
The tone should feel approachable and smart, not corporate. Optimized for
product-led or PLG-adjacent motion.

## Recommended hybrid — Launch direction

### Why a hybrid is the right call

None of the four pure forms is optimal for a solo-founder agency launching with
a small but real client roster, a content flywheel in motion, and a conversion
objective in the first 30 days.

The hybrid takes:
- **Visual register:** Direction 1 (dark, product-confident, controlled)
- **Proof density and CTA rhythm:** Direction 2 (logos high, repeated CTA, result-first)
- **Language and trust layer:** Direction 3 (no empty claims, process visible, support explicit)
- **Top-of-funnel hook:** Direction 4 (audit entry point, approachable sub-headline)

### Hybrid homepage structure

1. **Hero**
   - Headline: "Your competitors are already showing up in AI answers. Are you?"
   - Sub: "We help B2B companies become the source AI systems cite, recommend, and trust."
   - CTA primary: "Get your free GEO audit"
   - CTA secondary: "See how it works"

2. **Trust strip**
   - Logo row (4–6 logos, real or labeled "currently in audit")
   - One trust stat if available

3. **The shift (2 short paragraphs)**
   - AI search is changing who gets found. Most brands are invisible to it.
   - We fix that with a structured GEO system, not vague optimization.

4. **Service cards (3 cards)**
   - GEO Audit: "See exactly where and how AI cites your category — and where you're missing."
   - GEO Optimization: "Rewrite, restructure, and feed the signals AI systems need to surface you."
   - GEO Monitor: "Track your AI visibility weekly. Catch drops before they cost you."

5. **Sample deliverable preview**
   - Blurred or partial report card with "What you get" caption

6. **Case study (1 strong card)**
   - Company type + outcome + quote (real data or clearly marked example)

7. **Pricing (3 tiers)**
   - Audit only / Audit + Optimize / Full GEO retainer
   - Each with clear deliverables listed, not just tier names

8. **How we work (3 steps)**
   - Audit → Optimize → Monitor
   - Plain language, no jargon

9. **Support and QA block**
   - "What happens after you buy" — brief and concrete

10. **FAQ (5–7 questions)**
    - Focus on real objections: "Is this just SEO?", "How long before I see
      results?", "Who is this for?", "What do I get and when?"

11. **Final CTA**
    - "Launch my GEO audit" + short trust note
    - ("No commitment. Deliverable in 5 business days.")

### Build brief for victor-tech

Build the hybrid GEO Agency landing page using the structure above.

Visual register: dark (near-black background #0D0F12, white body text, cyan
accent #00C9C9 or similar). Typography: Inter or Geist, large hero (72px+),
controlled body (16–18px), strong section labels. Components needed: sticky
nav with CTA button, hero block (2 CTAs), logo strip, 2-column text block, 3
service cards row, blurred sample report card, 1 case study card, 3-column
pricing table with feature lists, 3-step process row, support block, FAQ
accordion, full-width final CTA banner. Mobile-first. No animation on first
paint. Lighthouse performance target: 90+. Static HTML or Framer for launch
(no CMS required). Flag all placeholder content with [PLACEHOLDER] tags.
